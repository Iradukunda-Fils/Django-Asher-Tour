ASHER SAFARI'S BEST CHOICE

Mountains

Rwanda’s landscape is defined by its rolling hills and towering peaks, including Mount Karisimbi, the highest at 4,507 meters. The Virunga Mountain Range is part of the country's breathtaking scenery, providing unique opportunities for trekking and wildlife viewing.

Wild Animals

Akagera National Park offers sightings of the Big Five (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino) and other wildlife like giraffes, zebras, and hippos. Nyungwe National Park is known for its primates, including chimpanzees and colobus monkeys, and a rich birdlife.

Gorillas

Rwanda is renowned for its mountain gorillas, with Volcanoes National Park being home to over half of the world’s population. Visitors can experience unforgettable gorilla trekking, contributing to conservation efforts and sustainable tourism.

Rwanda’s Ceremonies

Rwanda's ceremonies are vibrant celebrations that reflect the country's rich cultural heritage. They include traditional dances like Intore, cultural festivals, weddings, and religious events, all featuring music, dance, and community participation. National ceremonies, such as Heroes' Day and Genocide Memorial Day, honor history and resilience, reinforcing unity and respect.
